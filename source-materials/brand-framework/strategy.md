# Brand Framework — Strategy

**Source:** Purpose Media brand framework, Strategy section (5 pages)

---

## Brand Purpose

> To make it easy for people to take control of their debt without judgement, and find their way to a better financial future.

The purpose frames debt as an entry point, with financial wellbeing as the ultimate destination. It positions customers as active agents rather than passive recipients.

---

## Positioning Statement

> For people facing debt who feel stuck, overwhelmed or afraid to ask for help, PayPlan's financial guidance makes it genuinely easy to take back control. Free from judgement, backed by expert advice, and committed to a better financial future for everyone it works with.

This positions PayPlan between debt solutions and financial wellbeing, with a unique structural advantage: commercial agility combined with free consumer access.

---

## Three Foundational Truths

1. **People are capable, not vulnerable** — treating customers as adults in difficulty, not cases to manage
2. **Easy is a commitment, not a claim** — structurally designed for accessibility at every stage
3. **Debt is a chapter in the story** — relationships extend beyond debt resolution to long-term financial wellbeing

---

## Target Audience: Seven Readiness Stages

1. Pre-aware (early warning signs)
2. Passively aware (avoiding recognition)
3. Triggered (specific event catalyst)
4. Active, researching (committed to seeking help)
5. Active, deciding (validating provider choice)
6. In-plan, sustaining (navigating long-term solutions)
7. Post-solution (debt-free but financially uncertain)

---

## HEART Values Framework

| Value | Description |
|---|---|
| **Human** | Empathy, care, and respect — no jargon without explanation; real imagery; empathy before information |
| **Empowering** | Tools, guidance, and support — outcome-focused language; customers as capable adults; self-serve capability |
| **Ambitious** | Positive social and community impact — thought leadership on debt stigma; credible public positioning |
| **Real** | Clear, honest, and grounded — plain language throughout; proactive transparency on funding; addresses barriers early |
| **Transformative** | Technology and innovation — removes friction; people remove fear; mobile-first commitment |

---

## Eligibility Principle

> A brand that promises help without judgement cannot make that promise conditional on qualifying for a product.

Three eligibility gaps require distinct responses:

1. **Perceived ineligibility** — expand entry messaging to pre-crisis stages
2. **Conditional eligibility** — address complex cases (equity clauses, self-employed debt) proactively
3. **Structural ineligibility** — develop post-solution journey for debt-free customers like Diane Cooper

When PayPlan cannot offer a product, it must still offer a path: active warm referrals, emotional acknowledgement, and Breathing Space as a near-universal first gesture.
